Using some basic information you provide about yourself and your smoking behavior, this online tool uses data from the Prostate Lung Colorectal and Ovarian Cancer Screening Trial (PLCO) to give information about:

  • your chance of getting a lung cancer diagnosis (using the Lung Cancer Risk Assessment Tool (LCRAT))
  • your chance of dying from lung cancer (using the Lung Cancer Death Risk Assessment Tool (LCDRAT))
If you are interested in lung cancer screening, please visit our Risk-based NLST Outcome Tool (RNOT).

The best way to lower your risk of lung cancer, and all smoking-related diseases, is to quit smoking. Learn more by visiting smokefree.gov and the CDC page on quitting smoking or call 1-800-QUIT-NOW.
