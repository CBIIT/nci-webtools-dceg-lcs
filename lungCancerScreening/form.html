Using some basic information you provide about yourself and your smoking behavior, this online tool, the Risk-based NLST Outcomes tool (RNOT), uses data from the National Lung Cancer Screening Trial (NLST) to give information about:

  • whether someone like you would be recommended to get lung cancer screening by the US Preventive Services Task Force (a group of experts who make recommendations about prevention)
  • your chance of getting a lung cancer diagnosis
  • your chance of dying from lung cancer
  • your risk of a false alarm (a person has a positive screening test but does not actually have lung cancer)

The best way to lower your risk of lung cancer, and all smoking-related diseases, is to quit smoking. Learn more by visiting smokefree.gov and the CDC page on quitting smoking or call 1-800-QUIT-NOW.
