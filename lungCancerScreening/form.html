This tool uses basic information on smoking behavior and data from the National Lung Cancer Screening Trial (NLST) to determine the following:

  • whether someone like you would be recommended to get lung cancer screening by the US Preventive Services Task Force (a group of experts who make recommendations about prevention);
  • your chance of getting a lung cancer diagnosis in the next five years;
  • your chance of dying from lung cancer in the next five years;
  • your risk of a false-positive test result (a screening test result that indicates that a person has a specific disease or condition when the person actually does not have the disease or condition).

The best way to lower your risk of lung cancer, and all smoking-related diseases, is to quit smoking. Learn more by visiting smokefree.gov, the CDC quitting smoking page, or by calling 1-800-QUIT-NOW.
