Atlthough a patient's risk may be accurately estimated, these predictions do not allow one to say precisely which patient will develop colorectal cancer. In fact, some patients who do not develop colorectal cancer have higher risk estimates than some patients who do develop colorectal.

As a reminder, this tool was designed for use by health professionals. If you are not a health professional, you are encouraged to print these results and discuss them with your health care provider.

The tool uses a patient's medical history and the history of colorectal cancer among their first-degree relatives ( fathers, mothers, brothers, sitsers, sons, daughters) to estimate absolute colorectal cancer risk-their change of probability of developing colorectal cancer in a defined age individual

NIH ... Turning Discovery Into Health®

When we first developed this tool, we test it with white people and found it to be be accurate in estimating their risk of colorectal cancer. If you are Hispanic/Latino, this tool can still estimate your risk. But, because there is not as much data available fro these groups, your results may be less accurate.

In the future, we hope to make this tool more accurate for Hispanics/Latinos as researchers complete studies that will provide information to update the tool for these groups.

Although the tool is not perfect, the information you learn from using it can still help you and your doctor better understand your risk of colorectal cancer
