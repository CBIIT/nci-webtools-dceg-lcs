Personalized Results

Although a woman’s risk may be accurately estimated, these predictions do not allow one to say precisely which woman will develop breast cancer. In fact, some women who do not develop breast cancer have higher risk estimates than some patients who do develop the disease.

As a reminder, this tool was designed for use by health professionals. If you are not a health professional, you are encouraged to print these results and discuss them with your health care provider.

The tool uses the answers provided to estimate absolute risk of developing invasive breast cancer during the next 5-year period and up to age 90 (lifetime risk). This includes the woman’s personal medical and reproductive history and the history of breast cancer among her first-degree relatives (mothers, sisters, daughters). For comparison, the tool will then calculate 5-year and lifetime risk estimates for a woman of the same age who is at average risk for developing breast cancer.

Demographics

Patient & Family History

This tool cannot accurately calculate breast cancer risk for women with a medical history of any breast cancer or of DCIS or LCIS. See "About the Model and Source Code" for more information.

Other tools may be more appropriate for women with known mutations in either the BRCA1 or BRCA2 gene, or other hereditary syndromes associated with higher risks of breast cancer. See "About the Model and Source Code" for more information.

Assessments for Hispanic women are subject to greater uncertainty than those for white and African American women.

Researchers are conducting additional studies, including studies with minority populations, to gather more data and to increase the accuracy of the tool for women in these populations.
